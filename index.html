Scriabin's Best

The Best of Scriabin

Scriabin is considered to be one of the greatest composers of all time. Missing out on Scriabin because of his style is like not reading classic novels written in other languages because the translations don’t perfectly match modern English. Try and listen to his Etude Op. 8 No. 12 in D-sharp minor. It is filled with emotion and is easier to listen to than his more intense pieces. From there, I would move to his 24 Preludes Op. 11, as they start off sublime and turn more and more into the Scriabin that musicians fear. Finally, to get the true Scriabin experience, I would listen to his Le Poème de l'Extase, Op. 54 as this is the epitome of Scriabin’s influential and experimental music. To classical music enthusiasts, he is beloved and treasured; to most, he is unknown. For all music-lovers, Scriabin is worth a listen.

Best Pieces

  • Prométhée: Le Poème du Feu, op. 60: One of Scriabin’s greatest works. Showcases his signature 'mystic chord'
  • Le Poème de L'extase: A tone poem of epic proportions that shows off Scriabin's beautiful orchestrations and building of tension.
  • Vers La Flamme: A solo piano piece. The title means "into the flame". A showcase of Scriabin's masterful ability to produce movement and fire.
  • Etudes Op. 8, No. 12: A solo piano piece showcasing Scriabin's raw power and emotion.
  • Piano Concerto in F# minor: A beautiful love letter to composer Frederic Chopin.
